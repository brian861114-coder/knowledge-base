## Why this map exists

Biomaterials are not just softer versions of structural materials. They live where mechanics, chemistry, wear, and surface biology all attack at once.

## Map structure

[[Biocompatibility]] and [[Surface Engineering]] define the basic interface logic. [[Wear Resistance]] and [[Tribology and Wear]] explain why repeated contact can destroy a system that looked chemically stable on paper.

[[Corrosion Resistance]], [[Environmental Assisted Cracking]], and [[Hydrogen Embrittlement]] show how chemistry and stress collaborate to produce damage. [[Nanoindentation]] and [[Atomic Force Microscopy]] provide near-surface evidence layers useful when bulk tests miss the real mechanism.
