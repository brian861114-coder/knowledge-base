## Why this map exists

If characterization pages are left as a pile of unrelated instrument names, the knowledge base becomes useless. This map forces the methods to answer a harder question: what class of evidence each method can and cannot provide.

## Map structure

[[Microscopy Diffraction and Spectroscopy Map]] groups the methods that reveal structure, composition, phase, orientation, and bonding state. [[Mechanical and Environmental Testing Map]] groups the methods that force a response under load, temperature, impact, corrosion, or electrochemical bias.

[[Characterization and Failure Analysis Map]] ties measurement evidence back into damage interpretation. [[Thermal and Surface Analysis Map]] covers transitions, decomposition, surface chemistry, and bonding-sensitive methods that do not fit cleanly into pure imaging.

## Recommended order

Start with microscopy, diffraction, and spectroscopy if the problem is structural or chemical. Start with testing methods if the problem is performance or service response. Then use the failure-analysis map when you need to connect measurement back to an actual damage mechanism.
