## Why this map exists

Failure analysis without characterization is storytelling. This map keeps the knowledge base anchored to what can actually be measured.

## Map structure

[[X-ray Diffraction]] identifies phases, lattice spacing trends, and texture at a bulk scale. [[Scanning Electron Microscopy]] and [[Transmission Electron Microscopy]] reveal morphology and defects at smaller scales. [[Energy-Dispersive Spectroscopy]] adds fast local elemental contrast, while [[Electron Backscatter Diffraction]] adds orientation and grain-scale crystallographic context.

Those methods support interpretation of [[Corrosion]] and [[Fracture Mechanisms]]. [[Mechanical Testing]] closes the loop by converting structural evidence into load-response evidence. These methods all connect backward to [[Crystal Defects]] and [[Microstructure]], because most failures are structure-dependent rather than random events.
