## Why this map exists

Ideal crystal pictures are useful, but engineering materials do not live there. The structure that controls processing, properties, and failure is almost always defect-rich and interface-dominated.

## Map structure

[[Point Defects]], [[Dislocations]], and [[Planar Defects and Twins]] classify the main departures from perfect crystal order. [[Grain Boundaries]] and [[Interfacial Engineering]] deal with interfaces, which are often transport highways, crack deflectors, segregation sites, or reaction zones.

[[Phase Fraction and Morphology]] and [[Microstructure]] move from defect classes to structural state. [[Precipitation Hardening]] is one concrete example where deliberately controlled microstructural features change mechanical response.

## Core links

This map should be read together with [[Electron Backscatter Diffraction]] and [[Transmission Electron Microscopy]], because structural language only becomes rigorous when you know how those features are actually observed.
