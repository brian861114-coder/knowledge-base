## Why this map exists

Soft materials and electrochemical materials are often treated as separate worlds. That split is lazy. Both are dominated by interfaces, transport limits, rate dependence, and stability under operating environments rather than by one clean static property.

## Map structure

[[Polymer Viscoelasticity]] and [[Viscoelastic Response]] explain why polymer behavior depends on time scale, temperature, and loading history. [[Polymer Processing]] then shows how that behavior becomes an actual manufacturing constraint.

[[Electrochemical Stability]] and [[Ionic Conductivity]] form the electrochemical base. [[Solid Electrolytes]], [[Cathode Materials]], and [[Anode Materials]] live downstream of those constraints, while [[Electrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy]] provides one of the main evidence layers used to interpret whether transport or interfaces are failing.
