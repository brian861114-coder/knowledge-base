## Why this map exists

Environmental degradation is where material selection failures often hide. A part can be strong enough, tough enough, and still fundamentally wrong for the chemistry it sees.

## Map structure

[[Corrosion]] is the base concept, but it must be split into more specific routes such as [[Pitting and Localized Corrosion]] and [[Stress Corrosion Cracking]]. [[Environmental Assisted Cracking]] and [[Hydrogen Embrittlement]] cover cases where chemistry and mechanics directly reinforce each other.

[[Corrosion Fatigue]] and [[Thermal Fatigue]] are included because service failures often involve more than one damage driver at once. [[Corrosion Testing]] and [[X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy]] appear here because environmental failure diagnosis depends heavily on surface and interface evidence.
