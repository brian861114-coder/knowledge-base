## Why this map exists

Cracks are not all the same. A ductile overload failure, a brittle cleavage event, and a delamination problem may all be called "fracture," but they are not the same physical story.

## Map structure

[[Fracture Mechanisms]] gives the general framework. [[Ductile Fracture]] and [[Brittle Fracture]] split the two classic limits of crack-tip behavior. [[Interfacial Delamination]] handles systems where the weakest link is an interface rather than the bulk phase.

[[Fatigue]] is included because cyclic damage is one of the most common routes from small crack to catastrophic rupture. [[Fracture Toughness]] is the property lens, while [[Scanning Electron Microscopy]] and [[Mechanical Testing]] are part of the evidence stack used to diagnose what actually happened.
