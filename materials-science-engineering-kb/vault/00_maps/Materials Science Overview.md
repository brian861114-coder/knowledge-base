## Why this map exists

This map gives the field its backbone. Materials science is not just a list of material families. It is a causal network in which bonding and thermodynamics shape structure, processing alters structure, structure controls properties, and properties determine performance in service.

## Map structure

[[Atomic Bonding and Cohesion]] and [[Phase Equilibria and Phase Diagrams]] sit near the theoretical base because they explain why phases form and why certain structures are stable. [[Crystal Structure]], [[Crystal Defects]], and [[Microstructure]] translate those principles into concrete structural descriptions.

[[Mechanical Properties]], [[Electrical Properties]], and [[Thermal Properties]] are the decision-facing property notes. [[Heat Treatment]], [[Sintering]], [[Thin Film Deposition]], and [[Additive Manufacturing]] show how engineers actually move those properties.

[[X-ray Diffraction]], [[Scanning Electron Microscopy]], [[Transmission Electron Microscopy]], and [[Electron Backscatter Diffraction]] are not decoration. They are the evidence layer that prevents vague claims about structure from turning into fiction.

## Recommended order

Start with [[Atomic Bonding and Cohesion]], then move to [[Crystal Structure]], [[Crystal Defects]], and [[Microstructure]]. After that, connect into [[Mechanical Properties]] and one process note such as [[Heat Treatment]]. Only then should you branch into application clusters such as [[Battery Materials]] or [[Biomaterials]].
