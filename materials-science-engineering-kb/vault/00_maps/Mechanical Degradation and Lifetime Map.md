## Why this map exists

Static strength is not enough. Real components fail over time, under repeated load, under elevated temperature, or after damage has quietly accumulated below the usual yield-based design thresholds.

## Map structure

[[Strengthening Mechanisms]] explains how engineers push strength upward through grain size control, dislocation management, solid-solution effects, and second phases. Those gains are useful, but they also alter fracture sensitivity and cyclic behavior.

[[Fracture Toughness]] sits at the boundary between apparent strength and actual damage tolerance. [[Fatigue Resistance]] and [[Creep Resistance]] describe how life degrades under repeated loading or sustained high-temperature load, while [[Fatigue]] and [[Creep]] explain the underlying failure modes.

[[Thermomechanical Processing]] matters because it is one of the main levers for building the microstructures that control lifetime rather than just initial strength.
