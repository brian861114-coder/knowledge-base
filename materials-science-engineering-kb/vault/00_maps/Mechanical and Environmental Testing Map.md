## Why this map exists

Not every important measurement is about imaging structure. Many of the decisive methods force the material to respond under a controlled condition and then quantify how it survives, deforms, degrades, or fails.

## Map structure

[[Tensile Testing]], [[Hardness Testing]], and [[Impact Testing]] anchor the basic mechanical test family. [[Nanoindentation]] extends that logic into local, coated, or thin modified surface regions.

[[Corrosion Testing]] and [[Electrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy]] quantify environmental and electrochemical response. [[Differential Scanning Calorimetry]] and [[Thermogravimetric Analysis]] cover thermal transitions, reaction, and stability.

## Core links

These methods matter because they translate structure and chemistry into measurable performance. Their outputs become much more defensible when read together with structural methods rather than treated as standalone truth.
