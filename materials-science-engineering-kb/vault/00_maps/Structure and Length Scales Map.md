## Why this map exists

Students often learn structure as disconnected vocabulary: lattice, grain, defect, interface, texture, phase. That is the wrong mental model. These are not separate chapters. They are nested structural descriptions at different scales.

## Map structure

[[Unit Cell and Crystal Symmetry]] and [[Crystal Structure]] sit at the atomic scale, where periodic arrangement, coordination, and symmetry first appear. [[Amorphous Structure]] is not an afterthought; it is the alternative case where short-range order exists without long-range translational periodicity.

[[Point Defects]], [[Dislocations]], and [[Planar Defects and Twins]] describe departures from ideal order. [[Grain Boundaries]] and [[Phase Fraction and Morphology]] move upward into mesoscale structure. [[Microstructure]] then collects these ingredients into the structural state engineers actually observe and manipulate.

[[Texture and Anisotropy]] and [[Hierarchical Structure Across Scales]] explain why a material's response depends not just on what features exist, but also on how they are arranged across direction and scale.
