## Why it matters

Many engineering failures start at the contact, not in the bulk. Ignoring the contact problem is how supposedly strong materials grind themselves to death.

## Core relation

Real contact area, surface roughness, adhesion, plasticity, lubrication state, and third-body debris all control friction and wear evolution. The relevant stress state at the interface can be nothing like the bulk-average stress.
