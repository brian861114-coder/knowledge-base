## Why it matters

At high temperature, a material can fail while the nominal stress still looks acceptable. That is exactly why creep is dangerous: it makes room-temperature intuitions worthless.

## Core relation

Creep arises when time-dependent deformation pathways such as diffusion flow, dislocation climb, and grain-boundary sliding become active enough to accumulate strain under sustained stress. The dominant mechanism changes with temperature, stress, grain size, and phase stability.

## Links

[[Creep Resistance]] is the property-level summary of how strongly a system suppresses these mechanisms. [[Grain Boundaries]] matter because they can either block or accelerate creep depending on regime. [[High-Temperature Alloys]] are designed around this problem more than almost anything else.
