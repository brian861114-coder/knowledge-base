## Why it matters

In electrochemical systems, instability is not a side effect. It is usually the main reason a promising material stops being usable.

## Core relation

Stability depends on chemical driving force, transport, passivation behavior, and the local electronic environment at interfaces. A material can appear stable in bulk while its interface quietly decomposes under the actual operating potential.

## Links

[[Solid Electrolytes]] live or die by electrochemical compatibility with adjacent electrodes. [[Surface Engineering]] often exists to widen the usable stability window rather than to change the bulk material itself.
