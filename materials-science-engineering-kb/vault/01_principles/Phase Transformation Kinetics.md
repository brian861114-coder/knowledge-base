## Why it matters

Equilibrium tells you what is possible. Kinetics tells you what you will actually get before your process ends. That difference is the whole game in many real systems.

## Core relation

Phase change requires nucleation and growth, both of which depend on driving force, interfacial cost, and transport pathways. Cooling rate, hold time, and existing defects can therefore redirect a material into very different microstructures.

## Links

[[Heat Treatment]] and [[Additive Manufacturing]] both rely on path-dependent transformations rather than equilibrium alone. [[Microstructure]] records that path in the form of grain size, second phases, segregation, and texture.
