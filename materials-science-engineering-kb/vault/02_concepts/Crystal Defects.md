## Why it matters

Real materials are full of defects. The useful question is not whether defects exist, but which defects dominate behavior under the current condition.

## Core idea

Defects break ideal periodicity. Some defects accelerate transport, some enable plastic flow, some trap charge carriers, and some initiate cracks or corrosion.

## Links

[[Diffusion in Solids]] often proceeds through defect-mediated pathways. [[Fracture Mechanisms]] depends on how defects concentrate stress or redirect crack paths. [[Electron Backscatter Diffraction]] helps reveal grain and orientation structures connected to defect populations.

## Key examples

Vacancies are central to substitutional diffusion. Dislocations enable plastic deformation in most crystalline metals. Grain boundaries can strengthen a material at one temperature and accelerate creep or corrosion at another. This is why "defect" is a structural class, not a single effect.

## Common mistakes

Do not speak as if fewer defects always means better performance. Some properties improve when certain defects are introduced deliberately, while others collapse if the wrong defect class dominates. The correct question is which defect population is active and under what condition.
