## Why it matters

Structure is not cosmetic. It determines which deformation pathways are available, what defects cost, and how signals from diffraction or microscopy should be interpreted.

## Core idea

Crystalline materials are organized by lattice symmetry and basis. Different structures support different packing density, slip systems, diffusion behavior, and anisotropy.

## Links

[[Crystal Defects]] only make sense relative to an underlying crystal arrangement. [[X-ray Diffraction]] identifies phases and lattice information through structure-dependent diffraction patterns.

## Key examples

FCC, BCC, and HCP are not just labels to memorize. FCC structures often allow many easy slip pathways and therefore support ductility. BCC structures can be strong and temperature-sensitive in their plastic response because the available slip behavior is more complicated. HCP structures often show stronger directional dependence because the number and activity of slip systems are more limited at ordinary temperature.

## Common mistakes

Do not confuse crystal structure with chemistry alone. The same elements can appear in different structures under different conditions, and the same structure type can host very different chemistries. Also, a structure label by itself does not tell you grain size, texture, defect density, or phase fraction.
