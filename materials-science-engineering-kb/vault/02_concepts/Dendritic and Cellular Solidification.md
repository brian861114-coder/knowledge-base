## Why it matters

Many cast or rapidly solidified structures are shaped less by equilibrium phase labels than by how the interface actually grew through the liquid.

## Core idea

When a planar solidification front becomes unstable, it can break into cellular or dendritic morphologies. This happens because the coupled thermal and composition fields ahead of the interface change local growth conditions. The resulting arm spacing, segregation pattern, and interdendritic regions strongly affect later homogenization, precipitation, porosity, and cracking behavior.
