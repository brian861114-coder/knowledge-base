## Why it matters

High-entropy alloys matter because they force materials engineers to stop thinking in simple binary or ternary design habits. They are not magical, but they do expose where our intuition becomes too narrow.

## Core idea

These alloys use compositionally rich spaces to tune phase stability, lattice distortion, defect interactions, and environmental response. The payoff is potential access to unusual tradeoff combinations, not automatic superiority.
