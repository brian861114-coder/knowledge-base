## Why it matters

Classification is not just for organization. It gives a first-pass expectation for what structures, defects, properties, and manufacturing constraints are likely.

## Core idea

Metals, ceramics, polymers, composites, and semiconductors each occupy characteristic regions of the structure-property-processing space. Those families overlap, but the grouping remains useful because it predicts typical tradeoffs.
