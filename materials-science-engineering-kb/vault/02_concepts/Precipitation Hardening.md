## Why it matters

Precipitation hardening is one of the most effective examples of turning thermal history into strength. It is also a reminder that strength improvements are often metastable and temperature-sensitive.

## Core idea

A controlled heat-treatment route creates fine dispersed precipitates inside a matrix. Those precipitates raise strength by obstructing dislocation motion, but overaging or coarsening can erode the effect.
