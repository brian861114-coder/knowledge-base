## Why it matters

For high-temperature service, creep resistance is often more decisive than room-temperature strength. A material that slowly deforms out of tolerance is still a failure, even before it fractures.

## Definition

Creep resistance captures resistance to strain accumulation, damage evolution, and life loss under sustained stress at high temperature. Design usually cares about both creep rate and time to rupture.

## What controls it

Diffusion pathways, precipitate stability, grain structure, phase stability, and temperature exposure all matter. High-temperature materials are often designed around slowing microstructural change as much as around initial strength.
