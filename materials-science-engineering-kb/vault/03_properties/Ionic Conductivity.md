## Why it matters

In electrochemical materials, poor ionic transport can make a chemically attractive material functionally useless.

## Definition

Ionic conductivity quantifies ion transport through a bulk phase or network under a potential or concentration gradient. It is especially important for electrolytes, mixed conductors, and some defect-rich oxides.

## What controls it

Defect concentration, migration pathways, temperature, phase purity, interface resistance, and microstructural connectivity all matter. High conductivity in the bulk does not rescue a system with catastrophic interfacial resistance.
