## Why it matters

Optical behavior is often a direct readout of electronic structure, defects, interfaces, and morphology. It is therefore both a functional target and a structural diagnostic.

## Definition

This cluster includes transparency, absorption, reflectivity, refractive response, emission behavior, and scattering. The relevant metric depends on whether the material is being used for optics, photovoltaics, sensing, coatings, or thermal-radiative control.

## What controls it

Electronic states, bonding, defects, grain size, porosity, interface roughness, and nanoscale geometry all matter. Thin films and nanomaterials are especially sensitive because interference, confinement, and surface effects become prominent.
