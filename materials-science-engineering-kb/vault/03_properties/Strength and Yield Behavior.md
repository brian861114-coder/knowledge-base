## Why it matters

This is one of the first filters in engineering selection, but it is also one of the most misused because people love quoting yield strength as if it solved the whole design problem.

## Definition

Yield behavior marks the onset of significant permanent deformation, while strength metrics such as ultimate strength describe the maximum sustained load under a given testing mode. These are not universal constants; they depend on loading mode, temperature, strain rate, and structural state.

## What controls it

Dislocation motion, phase distribution, grain size, precipitates, porosity, and residual stress all matter. [[Strengthening Mechanisms]] raise strength by impeding deformation, but often at the cost of ductility, toughness, or thermal stability.
