## Why it matters

Thermoelectrics are one of the cleanest examples of why optimizing a single property is not enough. Improving one transport channel often damages another.

## Definition

This note focuses on the coupled balance among carrier transport, heat transport, and overall conversion-relevant behavior. The central issue is that electrical conductivity, carrier concentration, Seebeck-related response, and thermal conductivity are linked rather than independently adjustable.

## What controls it

Band structure, defect chemistry, carrier scattering, phonon scattering, grain boundaries, and nanoscale interfaces all matter. Many design strategies try to reduce heat transport without destroying useful electronic transport, but the levers are strongly coupled.
