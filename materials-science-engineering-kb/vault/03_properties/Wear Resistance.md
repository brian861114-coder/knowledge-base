## Why it matters

Surface loss can kill function long before a part fractures. Wear is often a geometry and interface failure before it becomes a bulk strength problem.

## Definition

Wear resistance measures the ability to limit material removal, surface damage, or friction-driven degradation under the relevant contact mode and environment.

## What controls it

Hardness, toughness, lubrication, roughness, debris formation, coating adhesion, and environment all matter. [[Interfacial Delamination]] can dominate when a coating or surface layer loses adhesion. High hardness alone is not a universal answer because brittle surfaces can fail catastrophically under the wrong contact.
