## Why it matters

Additive manufacturing is not just another shaping route. It often creates microstructures and defect populations that would be hard to reach through conventional processing.

## Workflow

Material is deposited or selectively fused in layers while thermal history evolves locally and repeatedly. Parameter windows are narrow because geometry, heat flow, and transformation kinetics interact strongly.

## Structure impact

Residual stress, porosity, anisotropy, melt-pool history, and rapid solidification features can dominate final behavior. Many of those features are directly tied to [[Solidification and Microsegregation]] and retained [[Residual Stress]]. That makes characterization and failure analysis mandatory rather than optional.
