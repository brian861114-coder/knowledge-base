## Why it matters

Annealing is the classic demonstration that heat can do more than soften a material. It can reset structural history, erase work hardening, and rebuild grain structure.

## Workflow

The route typically heats the material into a temperature-time window chosen to favor recovery, recrystallization, stress relief, ordering, or controlled coarsening, then cools in a way that preserves the intended state.

## Structure impact

Annealing changes dislocation density, substructure, residual stress, grain size, and sometimes texture. Because of that, it can restore ductility, reduce strength, alter conductivity, or improve dimensional stability depending on the prior state and the thermal schedule.
