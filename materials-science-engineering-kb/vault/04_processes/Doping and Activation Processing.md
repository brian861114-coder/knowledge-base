## Why it matters

A dopant written on a process sheet is not yet an electrically useful dopant. It has to land in the right site, survive the process, and remain compatible with the surrounding structure.

## Workflow

Typical routes include in situ doping during growth, diffusion, implantation-style introduction, and post-process thermal activation. The route depends on the material system and depth-control demands.

## Structure impact

These processes alter local composition, residual damage, defect populations, and interface chemistry. A badly chosen activation route can create more traps than useful carriers.
