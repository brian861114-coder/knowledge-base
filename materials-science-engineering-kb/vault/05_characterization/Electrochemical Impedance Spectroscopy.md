## Why it matters

EIS is one of the few practical tools that can tell you whether a system is limited by bulk transport, interface kinetics, or some ugly combination of both.

## What it measures

The method applies a small perturbation across frequency and reads the system's complex response. Different frequency regimes often correspond to different electrochemical or transport processes.

## Interpretation

Equivalent-circuit fits are useful but dangerous when treated as proof rather than models. EIS only becomes convincing when its assignments align with materials structure and other measurements.
