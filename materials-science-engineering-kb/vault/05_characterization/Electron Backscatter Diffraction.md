## Why it matters

EBSD is one of the clearest ways to connect grain-scale structure to process history and anisotropic behavior.

## What it measures

The method interprets diffraction patterns formed by backscattered electrons to infer local crystal orientation and phase information across a scanned surface. That makes it especially useful for grain structure, boundary character, and texture mapping.

## Interpretation

EBSD is excellent for grain maps, texture, and boundary characterization, but it depends on surface quality and does not directly reveal every defect class. Pair it with [[Scanning Electron Microscopy]] and, when needed, [[Transmission Electron Microscopy]]. It is one of the most useful methods for turning [[Texture and Anisotropy]] and [[Grain Boundaries]] from abstract concepts into measured structure.
