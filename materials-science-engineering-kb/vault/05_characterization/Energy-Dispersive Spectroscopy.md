## Why it matters

EDS is one of the fastest ways to stop guessing about whether a feature is chemistry-driven or just imaging contrast.

## What it measures

The method detects characteristic X-rays generated by electron-beam interaction with the sample and uses them to infer elemental composition or spatial compositional contrast. In practice it is usually paired with [[Scanning Electron Microscopy]] and sometimes with [[Transmission Electron Microscopy]].

## Interpretation

EDS is useful, but it is not magic. Spatial resolution depends on interaction volume, sensitivity varies by element, and the method is much stronger for elemental contrast than for precise chemical-state determination. If you need oxidation state or bonding environment, use [[X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy]] instead of pretending EDS can do that job.
