## Why it matters

Hardness is one of the fastest practical checks for whether processing actually changed the material the way you claimed.

## What it measures

The method indents the material using a defined geometry and loading protocol, then converts the residual or instrumented response into a hardness value. It is widely used for metals, ceramics, coatings, and heat-treated systems because it is fast and comparative.

## Interpretation

Hardness is useful because it is cheap and local. It is dangerous for the same reason. It does not directly replace tensile, fracture, or fatigue data, and surface condition or coating thickness can distort the result badly if you ignore measurement context.
