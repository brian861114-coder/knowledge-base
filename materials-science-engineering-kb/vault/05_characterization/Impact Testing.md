## Why it matters

Some materials look acceptable under slow loading and then fail embarrassingly under sudden impact. Impact testing exists to expose that difference.

## What it measures

The method subjects a defined specimen, often with a notch, to rapid loading and records absorbed energy or related dynamic response metrics. It is especially useful for comparing transition behavior, notch sensitivity, and relative toughness trends.

## Interpretation

Impact values are comparative, geometry-sensitive, and not a universal substitute for fracture mechanics. They are still valuable because they expose brittle transition behavior and fast-loading sensitivity that slow tests can hide.
