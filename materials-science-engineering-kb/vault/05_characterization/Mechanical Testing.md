## Why it matters

Claims about strength, ductility, toughness, fatigue life, or creep life are meaningless without a loading protocol that defines what was actually measured.

## What it measures

Mechanical testing includes tensile, compression, hardness, fracture, fatigue, and creep protocols. Each one probes a different part of the deformation and failure space rather than a universal material truth.

## Interpretation

Test output only means something inside its geometry, temperature, strain-rate, environment, and loading-history context. Treating one mechanical test as a complete description of performance is lazy and usually wrong. Use [[Tensile Testing]] for baseline load response, [[Hardness Testing]] for local resistance and processing-state checks, and [[Impact Testing]] when fast-loading sensitivity matters.
