## Why it matters

If a material loses mass, gains oxygen, or decomposes under heat, TGA will usually expose that more honestly than vague thermal-stability claims.

## What it measures

The method tracks mass versus temperature or time under controlled atmosphere. It is useful for oxidation, decomposition, solvent loss, binder burnout, and environmental stability analysis.

## Interpretation

Atmosphere, heating rate, sample geometry, and coupled reaction steps matter. TGA is often most useful when paired with DSC or spectroscopy rather than used in isolation.
