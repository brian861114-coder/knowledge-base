## Why it matters

When the real action happens at defect or interface scale, TEM is often the tool that stops speculation.

## What it measures

TEM transmits electrons through a thin specimen to reveal internal structure at very small scales. It can expose lattice defects, precipitates, interfaces, and local crystallographic information, and it can also be paired with [[Energy-Dispersive Spectroscopy]] for local chemistry.

## Interpretation

The method is powerful but sample preparation is demanding and field of view is limited. It is therefore best used to answer sharp questions, not to replace broader structural surveys.
