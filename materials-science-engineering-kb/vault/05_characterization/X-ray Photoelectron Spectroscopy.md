## Why it matters

When the problem is a few nanometers deep, bulk composition data is a distraction. XPS exists to stop you from lying to yourself about surface chemistry.

## What it measures

XPS measures binding-energy-resolved photoelectron signals from the near-surface region, enabling identification of elements, oxidation states, and some bonding environments.

## Interpretation

It is surface-sensitive, not universally representative. That is exactly why it is valuable. The method becomes powerful when its chemical-state assignments are tied back to processing and device or corrosion behavior.
