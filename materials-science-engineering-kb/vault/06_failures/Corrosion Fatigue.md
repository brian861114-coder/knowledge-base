## Why it matters

Corrosion fatigue punishes the lazy habit of analyzing fatigue in dry air and corrosion under static exposure, then assuming the results can simply be added together.

## Core idea

The environment can accelerate crack initiation by damaging protective films or creating pits, and it can accelerate crack growth by altering crack-tip chemistry or reducing ductility locally. The result is faster life loss than would be expected from either fatigue or corrosion acting alone.

## Links

[[Pitting and Localized Corrosion]] often provides initiation sites, while [[Fatigue Resistance]] becomes effectively lower in corrosive service than in laboratory air. [[Stress Corrosion Cracking]] overlaps in some regimes but is not identical because the loading history differs.
