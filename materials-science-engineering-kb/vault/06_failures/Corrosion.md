## Why it matters

Corrosion destroys components that look mechanically adequate on paper. It is a service-environment problem, not just a chemistry footnote.

## Core idea

Corrosion depends on thermodynamic driving force, kinetics, environment, and local structural conditions such as phase contrast, porosity, protective films, or residual stress.

## Links

[[Microstructure]] matters because local galvanic differences and interface structure can localize attack. [[Battery Materials]] and [[Biomaterials]] both treat surface stability as a primary design variable rather than an afterthought.
