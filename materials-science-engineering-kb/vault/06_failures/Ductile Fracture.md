## Why it matters

Ductile fracture is often more forgiving than brittle fracture, but it is still a failure mode. The presence of plasticity does not mean the structure was safe enough.

## Core idea

In ductile fracture, the crack tip is accompanied by significant plastic flow. Microvoids nucleate around inclusions, second phases, or highly stressed regions, then grow and coalesce until a macroscopic crack advances. This mechanism absorbs energy, which is why ductile failures often show higher apparent toughness than brittle ones.

## Links

[[Fracture Toughness]] often improves when the material can sustain extensive plastic deformation ahead of the crack tip. [[Scanning Electron Microscopy]] is a core diagnostic tool because ductile fracture surfaces often show dimpled morphology rather than cleavage-like facets.
