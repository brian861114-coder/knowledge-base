## Why it matters

Fatigue is one of the most common ways engineered parts die while everyone insists the nominal load was safe. The load can be safe once and still lethal a million times.

## Core idea

Cyclic loading nucleates and extends cracks through repeated local damage. Surface finish, inclusions, residual stress, and environment often matter more than broad alloy-category labels.

## Links
