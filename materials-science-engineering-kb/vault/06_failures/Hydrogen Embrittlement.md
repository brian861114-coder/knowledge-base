## Why it matters

Hydrogen embrittlement is a perfect example of why a decent-looking alloy and a decent-looking environment can still produce catastrophic nonsense together.

## Core idea

Hydrogen enters the material, redistributes under stress and defects, and reduces damage tolerance through mechanisms that depend on local structure and loading state.

## Links

[[Environmental Assisted Cracking]] overlaps strongly because both involve coupled chemistry and mechanics. [[Corrosion Resistance]] matters because the environment often governs whether hydrogen uptake becomes serious.
