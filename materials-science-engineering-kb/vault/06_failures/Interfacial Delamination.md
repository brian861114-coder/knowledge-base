## Why it matters

Interfaces are where many advanced material systems fail first. When the interface lets go, the impressive bulk properties often stop mattering immediately.

## Core idea

Delamination occurs when the interface cannot sustain the imposed combination of normal stress, shear stress, residual stress, environmental attack, or cyclic damage. The weakness may come from poor bonding, contamination, thermal-expansion mismatch, or progressive cracking near the interface.

## Links

[[Coatings and Protective Layers]] are vulnerable when adhesion and residual stress are poorly managed. [[Fiber-Reinforced Composites]] are vulnerable because interfacial load transfer and crack deflection both depend on interface quality.
