## Why it matters

Localized attack is much more dangerous than uniform thinning when failure depends on stress concentration and crack initiation. A small pit can matter more than a much larger amount of evenly distributed corrosion loss.

## Core idea

Localized corrosion begins when protective films break down or when local chemistry, inclusions, crevices, or galvanic contrasts create an unstable microenvironment. Once a pit stabilizes, it can acidify or concentrate aggressive species locally, making the attack self-reinforcing.

## Links

[[Stress Corrosion Cracking]] and fatigue failures often initiate from pits because pits act as sharp geometric and chemical stress concentrators. [[X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy]] and [[Energy-Dispersive Spectroscopy]] can help identify whether local chemistry or corrosion products differ from the surrounding surface.
