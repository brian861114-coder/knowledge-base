## Why it matters

High-temperature alloys expose whether a knowledge base actually understands stability over time or only understands room-temperature snapshots.

## Core idea

These alloys balance multiple competing goals: strength at temperature, resistance to coarsening, oxidation control, workable processing windows, and survivable defect populations. The design problem is brutal because every gain tends to destabilize something else.

## Links

[[Precipitation Hardening]] matters because many high-temperature systems rely on stable strengthening phases. [[Creep Resistance]] is the property lens, while [[Creep]] is the failure lens that reveals whether the design worked.
